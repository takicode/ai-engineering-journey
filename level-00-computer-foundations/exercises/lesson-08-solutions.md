Exercise 1

Explain the difference between:

A number
A representation of a number

A number is the abstract mathematical concept of a quantity or amount. It remains identical regardless of how you write it.A representation is the specific visual symbol, language, or code used to write that quantity down

Exercise 2

Convert to decimal:

101101

Show your working.
Working:
1 * 2^5 = 32
0 * 2^4 = 0
1 * 2^3 = 8
1 * 2^2 = 4
0 * 2^1 = 0
1 * 2^0 = 1
Summing the values:
32 + 0 + 8 + 4 + 0 + 1 = 45

Exercise 3

Convert to binary:

45

Show your working.

Working:
45 / 2 = 22 with a remainder of 1
22 / 2 = 11 with a remainder of 0
11 / 2 = 5 with a remainder of 1
5 / 2 = 2 with a remainder of 1
2 / 2 = 1 with a remainder of 0
1 / 2 = 0 with a remainder of 1
Result: Reading the remainders from the bottom up gives 101101

Exercise 4

Convert binary to hexadecimal:
11110000

Left group: 1111 in binary is 15 in decimal, which is F in hex.Right group: 0000 in binary is 0 in decimal, which is 0 in hex.Result: F0

Exercise 6 (Thinking)

Why do you think programmers prefer:

0xFFEE12

instead of:

111111111110111000010010
Even though they represent the same value?

Human readability: Reading 24 continuous ones and zeros is visually blinding. It is incredibly easy to miss a single digit or add an extra one by mistake.
Perfect 4:1 alignment: Exactly 4 binary digits compress into exactly 1 hexadecimal digit. This makes mental translation back and forth trivial.
